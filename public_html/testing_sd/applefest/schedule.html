Apple Pie Bake Off

Celebrity judge Emma Frisch, food network star to assist in selecting the winner of the first ever Apple Pie Bake Off at the 32nd Annual Downtown Ithaca Apple Harvest Festival

Competition opens to the public to put their baking skills to the test. Ever had a pesky neighbor who continues to talk about her tasty crust? Have them put their butter were their bragging is in this noon hour baking competition on Sunday of Apple Harvest Festival. Pies will be scored on Appearance, Crust, Filling and Overall Flavor/Taste. It is $10 to enter this bake off competition and the first place winner gains bragging rights, one Apple IPod Shuffle and a $50 cash prize. Other prizes and ribbons will be awarded to the runners up as well. For more information and competition rules visit Apple Harvest Festival at Downtown Ithaca on facebook or our main site. East End (200 & 300 North Aurora Street)

What is one interesting fact about Ithaca that visitors might learn while attending this festival? Ithaca was named by Bon Appetit Magazine as one of the foodiest towns in America and is home to the famous Moosewood Restaurant. Ithaca has more restaurants per capita than New York City and its unique placement as an urban center within an agricultural region allows for amazing farm to table cuisine. Our farmers market is one of the best in the country and the ice cream sundae was invented here! Apple Harvest festival embodies this spirit in bringing our culinary and agricultural values to the forefront.

Additional Community Events During Apple Harvest Festival

First Peoples' Festival

Dewitt Park Downtown

Saturday October 4, 2014, 10:00am - 5:30pm

This is the wonderful celebration of Indigenous peoples of New York State with traditional music, crafts, displays and foods. It provides an educational opportunity for the public to learn more about the traditions and unlearn the stereotypes connected with Indigenous cultures. Join us for our 5th Annual Celebration of Indigenous Culture. Featuring the Akwesasne Women's Singers in 2014. Children’s activities include Amazing Pete and his incredible balloon creations, water drums, Corn Husk Doll making, building projects led by Home Depot staff, face painting and more.

This event is sponsored by the CU American Indian Program, Ithaca College Center for the Study of Culture, Race & Ethnicity, IC Event Services, Cayuga Medical Center, Home Depot, Cornell Cooperative Extension TC, & Downtown Business Alliance. Funded in part by the Tompkins County Tourism Program. The First Peoples' Festival is in Dewitt Park in partnership with Ithaca's Apple Harvest Festival.

We welcome Indigenous vendors if interested please contact Audrey J. Cooper, 607-272-2292 or ajc39@cornell.edu.
